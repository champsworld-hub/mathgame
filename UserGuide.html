🧮 What is Number Ninja?

Number Ninja is a free, browser-based mental arithmetic game made by ChampsWorld. It trains the skill of holding a running total in your head as numbers appear one at a time on screen — exactly the mental workout used in competitive abacus (soroban) training.

The game is simple: numbers flash in sequence, you keep adding or subtracting as they appear, and at the end you type your answer. No pen. No calculator. Pure mental math.

It's designed for students, abacus learners, competitive math fans, and anyone who wants a sharper, faster brain. All you need is a Google account to sign in — the game itself is completely free.

🎮 How to Play

Everything lives inside five tabs across the top of the screen:

🎮 Play 🔢 Level 1 📊 Stats 📋 History 🏆 Challenge

Step-by-step

  • 1️⃣Go to Play, pick a difficulty (Easy / Medium / Hard / Custom), and tap Start Game!
  • 2️⃣Numbers appear one by one. Keep a running total in your head — add or subtract each new number as it arrives.
  • 3️⃣After the last number, type your final total and submit. You'll see instantly if you're right and how many points you earned.
  • 4️⃣Play more games to build streaks, climb your average score, and work towards harder levels.
💡 Window mode: In Settings you can turn on Show all numbers. This shows numbers in a sliding window of 5 — you tap Next to advance through them. Good for beginners who want a little more time to look.

⚙️ Custom Mode

Custom gives you full control for targeted training sessions.

  • 🔢Digits per number — slider from 1 digit (1–9) up to 8 digits. Use this to train huge-number addition.
  • 📋Number of rows — 2 terms up to 1010+. 20–50 rows makes for an intense workout session.
  • ✋Manual mode — you tap Next yourself to reveal each number. Best when learning a new technique.
  • ⏱️Auto mode — numbers advance automatically. Pick a speed: BG (Beginner) → LR → INT (Intermediate, 2 sec/number) → ADV → EX → 🥇 Champion.
  • 💾Save as preset — save your favourite config and reload it any time from the Load a preset… dropdown.

🔢 Level 1 – Abacus Technique Practice

The Level 1 tab drills the core abacus mental-math techniques one operation at a time, rather than random free-play.

Operation types

  • 🟢Direct — straightforward addition with no boundary crossing. Start here.
  • 🔵+5s — adding numbers that cross the 5-bead boundary (e.g. +1 → remove 4, add 5).
  • 🟠−5s — subtracting across the 5-bead boundary (e.g. −1 → add 4, remove 5).
  • 🟣+10s — adding numbers that cross the 10-bead (decade) boundary.
  • 🔴−10s — subtracting across the decade boundary.

Each type has sub-operations and shows a technique card with an explanation and memory tip. You also pick rows per question (2–7) to control how many numbers appear per round.

💡 How to use Level 1: Drill with 2 rows on Manual mode until the technique feels automatic, then increase rows and switch to Auto → Intermediate speed (2 sec/number).

📊 Stats

The Stats tab gives you a full picture of your progress over all games you've played.

  • ✅Accuracy — percentage of questions you've answered correctly overall, shown as a ring chart.
  • 🏅Total score, Avg score, Best score — your all-time scoring summary.
  • 🔥Best streak — longest run of correct answers in a row.
  • 📊Accuracy by Difficulty — bar showing your hit rate at each difficulty (e.g. 1d·3n = 1 digit, 3 numbers). Spot your weak points fast.
  • 📈Avg Score by Difficulty — compare performance across all difficulty levels.

The difficulty label format is Xd · Yn — X digits per number, Y numbers per question. So 2d · 7n is Hard mode.

📋 History

Every game you play is saved and shown in the History tab, newest first. Each entry shows:

  • ✅Whether you were Correct or wrong, and the exact number sequence shown (e.g. +8 +1 −4).
  • 🔢The correct answer and your answer — useful for reviewing mistakes.
  • ⏱️How long you took, how many points you earned, and the difficulty setting.

History is paginated (20 per page) and marked ● Live — new games appear in real time without refreshing.

🏆 Challenge Mode

Challenges let you compete against other players — typically a class of students or a group of friends — on the same set of questions.

  • ➕Tap + to create a challenge, or enter a code (e.g. NNJ-4X2K) given to you by a teacher.
  • ✅Complete the questions at your own pace. Accuracy and speed both count toward your score.
  • 🥈After finishing, see your Rank, how many questions you got correct, and how many students participated.
  • 📋Tap Past (top-right of the Challenge tab) to review all your previous challenge results.

⚙️ Settings

Tap your profile avatar in the top-left corner to open Settings.

  • 👤Nickname — the display name shown in the top-left during play. Your Google account name is stored separately and not editable here.
  • 📺Show all numbers (window mode) — when ON, numbers appear in a sliding window of 5 and you tap Next to advance. When OFF, numbers appear one by one on a timer.
  • ⭐Per-difficulty toggles — hide any difficulty tier (Easy / Medium / Hard / Custom) you don't want shown on the Play screen.
  • 💾Custom Training Presets — manage and rename your saved Custom configs. Tap Show to expand this section.

❓ FAQ

Is Number Ninja free?

Yes — completely free to play in any modern web browser. No download, no subscription, no ads.

Do I need an account?

You sign in with Google to save your stats, history, and presets. This is required — the game doesn't support guest play.

How is my score calculated?

Score depends on getting the answer right, how fast you answered, and the difficulty multiplier. A wrong answer always gives 0. See the Scoring Guide for the full breakdown with an interactive calculator.

What does "1d · 3n" mean in Stats?

d = digits per number, n = number of numbers per question. So 1d · 3n is Easy (3 one-digit numbers) and 2d · 7n is Hard (7 two-digit numbers).

What is the Level 1 tab for?

Level 1 teaches the specific abacus complement techniques (the mental shortcuts that make fast addition possible). If you're learning soroban or the Chinese abacus, start here before free-play.

What is Auto mode speed "INT"?

INT = Intermediate, which shows each number for 2 seconds before auto-advancing. It's a good daily-practice target. Faster speeds are ADV, EX, and Champion (🥇).

How do I join a Challenge?

Go to the Challenge tab, type the code your teacher or friend gave you (format: XXX-XXXX), and tap Join Challenge →.

Does it work on mobile?

Yes — the game is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets in any browser.

Is my data private?

We only store your name, email (from Google), age, and game scores to track progress. No ads, no data selling. See the Privacy Policy for full details.
