Who We Are

Number Ninja is a free educational math game designed to help children practise mental arithmetic. It is operated by [Ankita Agarwal / ChampsWorld] and hosted on GitHub Pages (Microsoft infrastructure) with data stored on Google Firebase.

Our source code is fully open and publicly auditable at github.com/champsworld-hub/mathgame — anyone can verify exactly what our site does.

2

What Data We Collect

Data Source Why We Need It
Parent name Google Sign-In Display & account identification
Parent email Google Sign-In Unique account ID + data archival communications
Child's age You enter it Benchmark your child's scores against age-group peers

We do not collect passwords, location, device identifiers, browser fingerprints, cookies for tracking, or any other personal information.

6

Children's Privacy

Number Ninja is designed for children but is accessed through a parent or guardian's Google account. By signing in and entering a child's age, the parent or guardian confirms they have read this policy and consent to the collection of their child's age and game activity data as described above.

We do not knowingly collect data from children without parental involvement. If you believe a child has used the app without parental consent, please contact us immediately and we will delete the data.

7

Data Retention

We keep game data for as long as your account is active. If your account has been inactive for 12 months, we will send an email notification before permanently deleting your data. You may also request deletion at any time (see Your Rights below).

8

Your Rights

As a parent or guardian you can at any time:

  • ✓ Access — request a copy of all data we hold about your account
  • ✓ Delete — remove all your data via Settings → Delete My Account, or by emailing us
  • ✓ Correct — ask us to fix any inaccurate information
  • ✓ Withdraw consent — delete your account at any time, which stops all data processing
